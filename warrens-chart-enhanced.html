THE WARRENS

Complete Guide to Paths of Power

Known Through Book III: Memories of Ice

Elder Warrens are the ancient, primordial sources of magic, predating the younger paths. They are more powerful but also more dangerous and difficult to access.

Younger/Aspected Warrens are derived from the Elder Warrens, accessible through study and practice. These are the paths typically used by mortal mages of the Malazan Empire and beyond.

THE HOLDS

The Holds are the ancient predecessors to the Warrens, even more primordial than the Elder Warrens. They represent the original paths of power before the Warrens were formed.

  • Ice Hold - Connected to Omtose Phellack
  • Dark Hold - Connected to Kurald Galain
  • Light Hold - Connected to Kurald Thyrllan
  • Shadow Hold - Connected to Kurald Emurlahn

The Holds are less understood than the Warrens and are considered nearly inaccessible to modern mages.

AZATH HOUSES

The Azath are mysterious houses that appear throughout the world, connected to both the Warrens and the Holds. They serve as prisons for beings of immense power.

Key Properties:

  • Trap and contain powerful entities
  • Connected to the ancient Holds
  • Can manifest in multiple realms
  • Nearly indestructible
  • Possess their own form of sentience

Known Azath: Deadhouse (Malaz City), the Azath in Darujhistan

IMPERIAL WARRENS

The Malazan Empire's High Mages primarily utilize the Younger/Aspected Warrens. Through the Imperial Cadre system, mages are trained in specific paths:

Commonly Used Imperial Warrens:

  • Thyr - Favored for destructive combat magic (light/fire)
  • Meanas - Used for illusion, deception, and covert operations
  • Mockra - Employed for interrogation and mental manipulation
  • Denul - Essential for military healers and support
  • Serc - Weather manipulation and naval warfare
  • Rashan & D'riss - Less common but available

The Empire cannot access Elder Warrens except through exceptional individuals. High Mages often learn multiple Warrens for versatility in combat.
